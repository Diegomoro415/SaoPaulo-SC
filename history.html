The youngest club among the greats of Brazilian football, São Paulo FC is the most winning club in Brazil!

Founded on January 25, 1930 and marked by stories of overcoming, such as the club's re-foundation in 1935 or the difficult times of building Morumbi, Tricolor Mais Querido on the planet is the only Brazilian club to have conquered América and the World for 3 times. He is also the only consecutive three-time champion of the Brazilian Championship and the first to win it six times. Brazilian three-time champion.

Off the field, Tricolor defied critics and logic and built the largest private stadium in Brazil, Estádio do Morumbi. It continued to be a pioneer in its commitment to infrastructure as a pillar of excellence in sport, building its Training Center and Athlete Training Center over time. The result? The world met three times.

Owner of an enviable heritage, holder of unattainable titles for many and in constant movement in search of excellence, Tricolor Paulista lives up to its anthem: among the greats, you are the first!

Greatest of all times

São Paulo Futebol Clube probably has the richest and most glorious history of national football. Filled with iconic players and hyper champions over the decades, the tricolor holds titles to date unmatched by its rivals more than centenarians, among them a three-time world championship, the record of 12 international titles conquered to date, making the club the only Brazilian Club among the 11 record holders on the planet.

Dream Team

Rogério Ceni

Period: 1990-2015

Main titles for the Club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1993 and 2005), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1993 and 2005), 1 Sud-American Cup (2012), 2 Recopa Sud-American (1993 and 1994), 1 Libertadores SuperCup (1993), 1 Conmenbol Cup (1994), 1 Conmenbol Master Cup (1996), 3 Brazilian league (2006, 2007 and 2008), 1 Rio-SP tournament (2001) and 3 Paulista (1998, 2000 and 2005) 1 Paulista SuperChampionship (2002).

O Mito (The Myth), as he is called by the crowd. Rogério Ceni, the greatest idol in the history of São Paulo Futebol Clube, is the ultimate personification of the tricolor fan on the field. Something that brings us back to the transcendent, to the magical and to everything that cannot be known and understood materially. With leadership and a lot of confidence, he defended our colors for 25 years and won 18 titles in total. He broke records for being the player with the most appearances for the same team and also the goalkeeper with the most goals in the history of world football. In total he played 1237 games and scored 131 goals.

Cafu

Period: 1988-1994

Main titles for the club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1992 and 1993), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1992 and 1993), 2 Recopas Sud-Americans (1993 and 1994), 1 Libertadores SuperCup (1993), 1 Brazilian leagues ( 1991) and 3 Paulista (1989, 1991 and 1992).

Cafu is one of the greatest left defender that Brazil has ever had, he was part of the national team group and won the World Cup (1994-2002) in addition to reaching second place in 1998.

Dario Pereyra

Period: 1977-1988

Main titles for the club: 2 Brazilian Leagues (1977 and 1986) and 4 Paulista (1980, 1981, 1985 e 1987).

Grit, technique, style, physical preparation... all in the purest Uruguayan spirit. That was Dario Pereyra, one of the greatest defenders in the history of his country and São Paulo, where he became an idol and played for over 10 years.
