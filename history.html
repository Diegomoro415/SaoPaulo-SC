The youngest club among the greats of Brazilian football, São Paulo FC is the most winning club in Brazil!

Founded on January 25, 1930 and marked by stories of overcoming, such as the club's re-foundation in 1935 or the difficult times of building Morumbi, Tricolor Mais Querido on the planet is the only Brazilian club to have conquered América and the World for 3 times. He is also the only consecutive three-time champion of the Brazilian Championship and the first to win it six times. Brazilian three-time champion.

Off the field, Tricolor defied critics and logic and built the largest private stadium in Brazil, Estádio do Morumbi. It continued to be a pioneer in its commitment to infrastructure as a pillar of excellence in sport, building its Training Center and Athlete Training Center over time. The result? The world met three times.

Owner of an enviable heritage, holder of unattainable titles for many and in constant movement in search of excellence, Tricolor Paulista lives up to its anthem: among the greats, you are the first!

Greatest of all times

São Paulo Futebol Clube probably has the richest and most glorious history of national football. Filled with iconic players and hyper champions over the decades, the tricolor holds titles to date unmatched by its rivals more than centenarians, among them a three-time world championship, the record of 12 international titles conquered to date, making the club the only Brazilian Club among the 11 record holders on the planet.

Dream Team

Rogério Ceni

Period: 1990-2015

Main titles for the Club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1993 and 2005), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1993 and 2005), 1 Sud-American Cup (2012), 2 Recopa Sud-American (1993 and 1994), 1 Libertadores SuperCup (1993), 1 Conmenbol Cup (1994), 1 Conmenbol Master Cup (1996), 3 Brazilian league (2006, 2007 and 2008), 1 Rio-SP tournament (2001) and 3 Paulista (1998, 2000 and 2005) 1 Paulista SuperChampionship (2002).

O Mito (The Myth), as he is called by the crowd. Rogério Ceni, the greatest idol in the history of São Paulo Futebol Clube, is the ultimate personification of the tricolor fan on the field. Something that brings us back to the transcendent, to the magical and to everything that cannot be known and understood materially. With leadership and a lot of confidence, he defended our colors for 25 years and won 18 titles in total. He broke records for being the player with the most appearances for the same team and also the goalkeeper with the most goals in the history of world football. In total he played 1237 games and scored 131 goals.

Cafu

Period: 1988-1994

Main titles for the club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1992 and 1993), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1992 and 1993), 2 Recopas Sud-Americans (1993 and 1994), 1 Libertadores SuperCup (1993), 1 Brazilian leagues ( 1991) and 3 Paulista (1989, 1991 and 1992).

Cafu is one of the greatest left defender that Brazil has ever had, he was part of the national team group and won the World Cup (1994-2002) in addition to reaching second place in 1998.

Dario Pereyra

Period: 1977-1988

Main titles for the club: 2 Brazilian Leagues (1977 and 1986) and 4 Paulista (1980, 1981, 1985 e 1987).

Grit, technique, style, physical preparation... all in the purest Uruguayan spirit. That was Dario Pereyra, one of the greatest defenders in the history of his country and São Paulo, where he became an idol and played for over 10 years.

Lugano

Period: 2003-2006 and 2016-2017

Main titles for the club: 1 FIFA Club World Cup (2005), 1 Copa Libertadores da América (2005), 1 Brazilian Championship (2006) and 1 Paulista Championship (2005).

"Dios Lugano" That's what the Uruguayan defender was called. Impeccable in marking, full of race, feared by rival's strikers, he was essential in winning the Club World Cup in 2005.

Leonardo

Period: 1990-1991, 1993-1994 and 2001

Main titels for the club: 1 FIFA Club World Cup (1993), 1 SuperCup Sud-Americans (1993), 2 Recopas Sud-Americans (1993 and 1994) and 1 Brazilian league (1991).

He had two great spells at the club. In the first, he helped to win the 1991 Brazilian league, in the second, he won the FIFA Club World Cup. Very technical, he was part of the national team, was champion in the 1994 world cup and runner-up in the 1998 world cup.

Mineiro

Period: 2005-2006

Main Titles for the club: 1 FIFA Club world Cup (2005), 1 Copa Libertadores da América (2005), 1 Brazilian league (2006) and 1 Paulista (2005).

He scored the most important goal in the club's recent history, the goal against Liverpool in the Club World Cup, Mineiro was tireless, skillful, a true lion in midfield. He joined the national team to play in the 2006 world cup before transferring to Europe where he played for Chelsea ENG.

Pedro Rocha

Period: 1970-1977

Main Titles for the club: 1 Brazilian league (1977) and 2 Paulistas (1971 and 1975).

Pedro Rocha, another Uruguayan who enchanted sports lovers with formidable exhibitions and a fascinating skills, which led to idol status in the giants Penarol and São Paulo and also in the Uruguayan national team.

Müller

Period: 1984-1988, 1990-1994 and 1996

Main Titles for the club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1992 and 1993), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1992 and 1993), 2 Brazilian league (1986 and 1991), Super Copa da Libertadores (1993) and 4 Paulista (1985, 1987, 1991 and 1992).

Simply infernal striker, goalscorer and fast. He scored 160 goals and was on the national team in 1986, 1990 and 1994 where he won the World Cup.

Leonidas da Silva

Period: 1942-1950

Main Titles for the club: 5 Paulista (1943, 1945, 1946, 1948 and 1949).

The undeniable legend! O Diamante Negro (The Back Diamond) the creator of the Bicycle Kick. It was he who forever transformed the history of São Paulo, with Leonidas the club made history and catapulted its history from then on.

Careca

Period: 1983-1987

Main Titles for the club: 1 Brazilian league (1986) and 1 Paulista (1985).

Top 10 of the greatest strikes in Brazilian football history, synonymous with quality and art on the field. The top scorer of São Paulo and also of the national team, Careca decided games alone, was one of the most spectacular strikers we have ever seen in Brazilian fields. He also made a memorial partnership with the genius Maradona at Napoli.

Raí

Period: 1987-1993 and 1998-200

Main Titles for the club: 1 FIFA Club World Cup (1992), 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1992 and 1993), 1 Brazilian league (1991) and 5 Paulista (1989, 1991, 1992, 1998 and 2000).

With an enviable range of qualities, Raí was one of the greatest idols in the history of São Paulo and one of the most complete midfielders in Brazil. With him on the field, and with Telê Santana as coach, São Paulo dominated the national and international scene in the early 90s, conquering everything they disputed.

Telê Santana

Period: 1973 and 1990-1996

Main Titles for the club: 2 FIFA Club World Cup (1992 and 1993) 2 Copa Libertadores da América (1992 and 1993), 2 Recopa Sud-American (1993 and 1994), 1 Libertadores SuperCup (1993), 1 Brazilian league (1991), 1 Conmenbol Cup (1994), 2 Paulista (1991 and 1992).

Mestre Telê, was the best coach Brazilian football has ever had, no other coach was so smart, so friendly, and so successful. Telê passed away in 2006 but the crowd never forgot him, his name is always remembered by the crowd that game after game keeps singing his song "Olêee, olê, olê, olê....Telê, Telê....".

Morumbi

Estádio Cícero Pompeu de Toledo, widely known as Morumbi, is the largest privately owned stadium in Brazil. The stadium construction was finally concluded in 1970. Stage of so much glory, Morumbi was for long time the largest private stadium in the world, the attendance record currently stands at 138,032 people but now, its maximum capacity is 67,052. Inside the stadium we have the Trophy Room, with photos, articles and all the trophies already won by São Paulo, highly recommended for game days you can arrive early and take your children for a visit.
